Volunteer Engagement

Volunteers are one of the most valuable resources available to any animal shelter. They provide enrichment, exercise, adoption support, photography, networking, and thousands of hours of unpaid labor that directly benefit animals.

2025 In-Shelter Volunteer Hours

Public records reviewed by advocates show Gwinnett Animal Shelter logged far fewer in-shelter volunteer hours than neighboring counties.

The Numbers Tell the Story

Volunteer hours translate into dog walks, enrichment, adoption promotion, rescue networking, event support, and additional human interaction for shelter animals.

When volunteer participation is low, animals lose critical support. Gwinnett County should be asking why one of Georgia's largest counties is generating so few in-shelter volunteer hours compared to neighboring shelters.

The Retention Cliff

Volunteer data also raises concerns about retention. Advocates estimate that approximately 68% of new volunteers logged fewer than five hours before disengaging.

Reported Barriers

Community members want to help. But when the process is slow, restrictive, or discouraging, animals lose valuable support.

Long Wait Times

Residents have reported waiting months between application, orientation, shadow shifts, and active volunteering.

Access Restrictions

Policies that limit dog walking, Dog Day Out, photos, videos, and networking reduce volunteer impact.

Limited Promotion

When volunteers cannot freely help promote animals, adoptable pets lose visibility with the public and rescues.

Communication Concerns

Volunteers have described feeling discouraged from raising concerns or offering constructive feedback.

What We Are Advocating For

Improve Access

Reduce barriers to entry and create more opportunities for community members to begin helping animals.

Support Collaboration

Encourage respectful communication between staff, volunteers, rescues, and community stakeholders.

Track Outcomes

Share volunteer participation, retention, and program outcome data so the community can help identify solutions.

Volunteers Should Be Partners in Lifesaving

Gwinnett County has an opportunity to build a volunteer program that welcomes participation, supports transparency, and maximizes positive outcomes for animals.
