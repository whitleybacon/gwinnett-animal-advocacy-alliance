The Dog Who Started a Movement

In July 2024, a dog named Pluto changed everything.

His euthanasia sparked questions, community involvement, public records requests, and conversations about transparency and accountability.

What began as concern for one dog grew into a movement focused on improving outcomes for all animals in Gwinnett County.

Pluto's story became the catalyst for what would eventually become Gwinnett Animal Advocacy Alliance (GAAA).

Who We Are

Gwinnett Animal Advocacy Alliance is a community-based advocacy organization dedicated to improving animal welfare in Gwinnett County, Georgia.

We believe meaningful change happens when residents stay informed, ask questions, hold public agencies accountable, and advocate for practical solutions that improve outcomes for animals.

Unlike shelters, rescues, or government agencies, our focus is on education, public engagement, transparency, and accountability.

Why We Exist

They Depend On Us.

Animals cannot attend meetings.

Animals cannot review reports.

Animals cannot request public records.

Animals cannot ask elected officials questions.

Animals cannot advocate for themselves.

That is why Gwinnett Animal Advocacy Alliance exists.
