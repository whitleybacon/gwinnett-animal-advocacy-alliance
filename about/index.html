Who We Are

Gwinnett Animal Advocacy Alliance (GAAA) is a community-based advocacy organization dedicated to improving animal welfare in Gwinnett County, Georgia.

We believe meaningful change happens when residents stay informed, ask questions, engage with local government, and advocate for practical solutions that improve outcomes for animals.

Unlike shelters, rescues, or government agencies, our focus is on education, public engagement, transparency, and accountability.

The Dog Who Started a Movement

In July 2024, a dog named Pluto changed everything.

His euthanasia sparked questions, community involvement, public records requests, and conversations about transparency and accountability.

What began as concern for one dog grew into a movement focused on improving outcomes for all animals in Gwinnett County, Georgia.

Pluto’s story became the catalyst for what would eventually become Gwinnett Animal Advocacy Alliance.

Why We Exist

Animals cannot attend meetings.

They cannot review reports.

They cannot request public records.

They cannot ask elected officials questions.

They cannot advocate for themselves.

They depend on people who are willing to pay attention, speak up, and work toward better outcomes.

That is why Gwinnett Animal Advocacy Alliance exists.
