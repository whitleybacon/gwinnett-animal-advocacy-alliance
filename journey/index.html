Our Advocacy Journey

Every improvement begins with someone willing to speak up.

Our Story

Since 2024, Gwinnett Animal Advocacy Alliance has worked to improve animal welfare through research, education, collaboration, and community advocacy.

Every milestone on this page represents countless hours spent attending public meetings, reviewing public records, communicating with county leadership, educating the community, and advocating for meaningful improvements on behalf of the animals.

Meaningful change takes time. While not every recommendation results in immediate action, every conversation, every public record reviewed, every recommendation shared, and every resident who joins our efforts helps move Gwinnett County toward a more transparent, accountable, and compassionate animal welfare system.

2024

July 2024

GAAA Is Founded

Pluto’s euthanasia inspired a group of concerned residents to come together with one goal: improving animal welfare in Gwinnett County.

  • Began attending Animal Advisory Council meetings
  • Started educating the community
  • Created the first GAAA website
  • Launched our first petition

September 2024

Raising Public Awareness

  • Created and distributed an animal welfare questionnaire to candidates running for the Gwinnett County Board of Commissioners
  • Began documenting concerns involving transparency, shelter operations, and animal outcomes
  • Raised concerns after volunteers lost the ability to photograph and video shelter animals

October 2024

First Meeting With County Leadership

Met with Commissioner Carden to discuss concerns involving shelter operations, transparency, and animal welfare.

2025

January 2025

Meeting With Commissioner Ku

Met with Commissioner Ku to discuss animal welfare concerns and opportunities for meaningful improvement.

February 2025

Meeting With Shelter Leadership

Met with shelter leadership to discuss community concerns, including:

  • Transparency
  • Shelter accessibility
  • Operating hours
  • Volunteer engagement

May 2025

Policy Recommendations

Continued discussing animal welfare issues with Commissioner Carden and advocated for stronger local solutions.

  • A ban on retail pet sales
  • Stronger breeding regulations
  • Expanded access to spay and neuter services
  • Preventive approaches to pet overpopulation

June 2025

Retail Pet Sales Resolution Requested

Contacted all Gwinnett County commissioners to request a resolution addressing retail pet sales in recognition of the one-year anniversary of Pluto’s death.

July 2025

State-Level Advocacy

Met with a field representative from the Office of Lieutenant Governor Burt Jones to discuss statewide animal welfare concerns.

  • Georgia’s animal shelter crisis
  • Backyard breeding and pet overpopulation
  • Access to affordable spay and neuter services
  • Minimum standards of shelter care
  • Animal cruelty laws and enforcement
  • Legal recognition of TNR

2025

Public Records and Research

Expanded the use of Open Records Requests and began reviewing shelter policies, records, and operational data to better understand:

  • Shelter operations
  • Animal outcomes
  • Policies and procedures
  • Areas needing improvement
2026

January 2026

Animal Death Analysis Published

Analyzed and published findings from the shelter’s 2025 animal death data, including euthanasia and unassisted deaths.

The analysis also examined reported reasons for dog euthanasia and raised questions about how overcrowding and shelter capacity affect animal outcomes.

January 2026

Medical Care Review Published

Published a detailed review of public medical records identifying recurring concerns involving treatment, monitoring, documentation, and accountability.

View Medical Care Findings →

February 2026

Volunteer Engagement Study

Analyzed 2025 volunteer participation and retention data and compared Gwinnett’s program with neighboring counties.

  • Reviewed total volunteer participation
  • Identified a significant early-retention drop-off
  • Documented barriers to volunteer involvement
  • Shared recommendations for improvement
View Volunteer Findings →

March 2026

Advocacy Win

Photo and Video Restriction Lifted

After approximately 18 months of sustained advocacy, volunteers regained the ability to photograph and video shelter animals for adoption and rescue promotion.

March 2026

Cameras and Independent Oversight

Launched a public petition supporting expanded camera coverage and an independent review of shelter operations.

Learn About Camera Coverage →

June 2026

Owner Accountability Raised

Contacted shelter leadership regarding accountability when known owners fail to reclaim animals left in shelter care.

July 2026

Expanded Website Launch

Launched a completely redesigned website with documented issue pages, public resources, leadership information, and opportunities for residents to take action.

July 2026

Canine Enrichment Audit Published

Completed an independent forensic audit of documented staff enrichment for every dog housed during May 2026.

View the Enrichment Findings →

July 2026

Community Momentum

  • Helped mobilize more than 50 residents to attend the July Animal Advisory Council meeting
  • Expanded public participation in local animal welfare discussions
  • Launched the first GAAA community T-shirt campaign
Ongoing Advocacy

Advocacy Happens Between the Headlines

While milestones mark important moments, much of our work happens behind the scenes.

Throughout our advocacy journey, we have consistently:

  • Communicated with county commissioners
  • Communicated with shelter leadership
  • Sent numerous emails and follow-up requests
  • Followed up when concerns went unanswered
  • Attended Animal Advisory Council meetings
  • Submitted Open Records Requests
  • Reviewed public records and shelter data
  • Shared documented findings with the public
  • Proposed practical recommendations
  • Encouraged residents to participate

Our communications and recommendations have addressed:

Managed Intake Volunteer Engagement Medical Care Transparency Community Cats Shelter Accessibility Canine Enrichment Animal Protection Policies

Our goal has always been collaboration and meaningful improvement for the animals.

Our Work Remains Focused On

Our approach combines public education, documented research, constructive advocacy, and community involvement.

Educate

Raising awareness and helping residents understand how local animal welfare systems operate.

Research

Reviewing public records, analyzing shelter data, and documenting areas that need improvement.

Advocate

Working with residents, decision-makers, and community partners to advance practical solutions.

Improve Outcomes

Promoting a more transparent, accountable, accessible, and compassionate animal welfare system.

The Journey Continues

Meaningful change does not happen overnight. Every meeting attended, every public record reviewed, every recommendation shared, and every resident who chooses to get involved makes a difference.

Together, these efforts help move Gwinnett County toward a more transparent, accountable, and compassionate animal welfare system.
